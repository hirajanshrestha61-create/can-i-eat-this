Food safety facts
Numbers that should
change your habits
2 hrs
The danger zone limit
The FDA and USDA agree: perishable food left between 4°C–60°C for more than 2 hours should be discarded. In temperatures above 32°C, the window drops to just 1 hour.
600M
People sickened by food yearly
The WHO estimates 600 million cases of foodborne illness occur globally each year — 420,000 of which are fatal. Most are entirely preventable with basic safety awareness.
20 min
Bacteria doubling time
In ideal conditions, a single bacterium can become 2 billion in just 10 hours by doubling every 20 minutes. This is why time and temperature matter so dramatically.
73°C
Safe internal temp for poultry
Cooking to 73°C / 165°F kills Salmonella, Campylobacter, and Listeria in poultry. For beef, pork and lamb, 71°C / 160°F. Use a thermometer — colour is not reliable.
